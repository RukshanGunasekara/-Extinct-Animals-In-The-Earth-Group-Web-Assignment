ANHANGUERA

The large genus Anhanguera (meaning "old devil") is one of the best-known large pterosaurs from the Aptian period of the early Cretaceous period. In appearance, it has an unusually large head, keeled crest and long, powerful wings, and the muscular torso of its fellow ornithocheirids. Anhanguera is a pterosaur, a type of flying reptile. Anhanguera lived in the early Cretaceous period and inhabited South America. The first Anhanguera fossil was discovered in 1985. They have eaten fish. Anhangura had long strong jaws and a crest on the top of the nose. This feature will give the anchor stability when fishing in the sea.
